Your friend Neil does research in DNA sequencing. Knowing you're taking a course in algorithms, he came to you for help on a problem, on which he says he's been stuck for days: given two sequences of DNAs, can you design an algorithm to tell how "similar" they are?

To make this problem more precise, we'd like to measure the similarity of two strings by computing their longest common subsequence. As a reminder, a subsequence of a string $S$ is any string that can be derived from the original by deleting some characters in $S$ without changing the relative order of any two characters.

Our input will be two strings, $A[1..n]$ and $B[1..m]$. Determine the length of the longest common subsequence between $A$ and $B$. The longest common subsequence may not be unique, but we're only interested in the length for this problem.

Example

If $A$="ACACTCGC" and $B$="CATGACAG", then one possible longest common subsequence is CATGC. You can check that it is not the unique common subsequence of length 5, but there are none of length 6.

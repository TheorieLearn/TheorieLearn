Let $G$ be a directed acyclic graph, in which every edge $e \in E$ has a weight $w(e),$ which could be positive, negative, or zero. More formally, for any path $P = v_0 \to v_1 \to v_2 \to \cdots \to v_\ell$ in $G$, the alternating length of $P$ is defined as

$$ AltLen(P) = \sum_{i=0}^{\ell-1} (-1)^i\cdot w(v_i \rightarrow v_{i+1}). $$

Describe an algorithm to find a path from $s$ to $t$ with the largest alternating length, given the graph $G$, the edge weights $w(e)$, and vertices $s$ and $t$ as input.

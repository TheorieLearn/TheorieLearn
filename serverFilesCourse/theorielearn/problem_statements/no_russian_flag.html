Consider a directed graph $G$, where each edge is colored either red, white, or blue. A walk in $G$ has no Russian flag if and only if there is no instance of a white edge, followed by a blue edge, then a red edge, within the walk. More formally, for any walk $v_0\rightarrow v_1 \rightarrow \ldots \rightarrow v_k$ has no Russian flag if, for every integer $i\in{0,\dots,k-3}$, the edge color for $v_i \rightarrow v_{i+1}$, $v_{i+1} \rightarrow v_{i+2}$, and $v_{i+2} \rightarrow v_{i+3}$ are not white, blue, and red, respectively.

Describe an efficient algorithm to determine whether a destination vertex $t$ is reachable from $s$ in a given edge-colored directed graph $G$ with no Russian flags.

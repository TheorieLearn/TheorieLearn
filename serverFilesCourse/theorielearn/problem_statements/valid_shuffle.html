A shuffle of two strings $X$ and $Y$ is formed by interspersing the characters into a new string, keeping the characters of $X$ and $Y$ in the same order. For example, the string BANANAANANAS is a shuffle of the strings BANANA and ANANAS in several different ways: BANANAANANAS, BANANAANANAS, or BANANAANANAS.

Describe and analyze an efficient algorithm to determine, given three strings $A[1..n]$, $B[1..m]$, and $C[1..n+m]$, whether $C$ is a shuffle of $A$ and $B$.

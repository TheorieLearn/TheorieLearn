Momo is planning a road trip along a highway with $n$ mile markers, numbered from $1$ through $n$. Momo is currently at mile marker $1$, and she would like to travel to mile marker $n$. She can only drive 1 or 2 miles forward on a single day.

The costs of hotels at each mile are provided in the array $HotelCosts[1..n]$.

Note: Momo does not have to stay in a hotel at mile $1$ and mile $n$. Thus, the first and last elements of $HotelCosts$ will always be 0. Also, Momo cannot stay in the same hotel two nights in a row.

We have a new update: Right before Momo leaves, she discovers a stash of $k$ coupons. Each coupon allows her to stay at a hotel for free.

Determine the minimum possible total cost of Momo's trip, given that she can stay in up to $k$ hotels for free.

Example

If $HotelCosts = [0, 128, 225, 1, 374, 3, 0]$ and $k = 1$, then one optimal itinerary is to stay overnight at mile markers 2, 4, 6 and use a coupon at mile 2, which costs $1+3=4$. Note that 3, 4, 6 (using a coupon at mile 3) is also optimal.

If $k = 2$ instead, then the (only) optimal itinerary is to stay overnight at mile 3 and 5 and use a coupon at both of those hotels; the cost of this trip would be 0.

Let $MinCost(i, j)$ denote the minimum possible cost of traveling from mile $i$ to mile $n$ (including the cost of the hotel at mile $i$) with $j$ remaining coupons.

Notice that the total cost of Momo's trip can be expressed as:

[The cost of the hotel at mile $i$] + [The cost of all hotels she will stay at after mile i]

Since we already know the cost of the current hotel, we need to derive an expression for the cost of all of the hotels she stayed at after this hotel. At this point, we can address the first step of the general problem-solving advice. The choice to consider is: what is the next hotel that Momo can stay at after staying at the hotel at mile $i$? Choose all that apply below.

Hotel at mile $i$ Hotel at mile $i - 1$ Hotel at mile $i - 2$ Hotel at mile $i + 1$ Hotel at mile $i + 2$ Any of the hotels at miles $1$ through $i - 1$ Any of the hotels at miles $1$ through $i - 2$ Any of the hotels at miles $i+1$ through $n$ Any of the hotels at miles $i+2$ through $n$

Recall that $MaxFun(v, o)$ is the maximum total fun values of a subset of employees chosen from the subtree rooted at $v$ where $o$ represents whether $v$ is included or not.

Now, we will address the first step of the above advice. When we are considering $MaxFun(v, o)$, notice that the problem breaks down into the following cases:

  1. $o = \text{Include}$, so vertex $v$ is included in our subset
  2. $o = \text{Exclude}$ so vertex $v$ is excluded in our subset
Let's analyze how our subproblem changes for each of those cases:


Case 1: If our solution has $o = \text{Include}$ so that vertex $v$ is included in the subset, what are the options of $o$ for all the children of vertex $v$? Include Exclude

Case 2: If our solution has $o = \text{Exclude}$ so that vertex $v$ is excluded from the subset, what are the options of $o$ for all the children of vertex $v$? Include Exclude

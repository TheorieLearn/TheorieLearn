Using $G'$ to Solve the Problem


Next, we need to use our constructed graph $G'$ to solve our problem. How do paths in $G$ and $G'$ correspond?

Let $P = v_0 \to v_1 \to \dots \to v_n$ be a path in $G$. Then, by construction, $G'$ contains the corresponding path $P' = (v_0,+) \to (v_1,-) \to \dots \to (v_n,\pm)$. Furthermore, the alternating length of $P$ in $G$ is equal to the length of $P'$ in $G'$. Let $P' = v_1 \to v_2 \to \dots \to v_n$ be a path in $G'$. Then, by construction, $G$ contains the corresponding path $P = v_0+ \to v_1 \to \dots \to v_n$. Furthermore, the length of $P$ in $G$ is equal to the alternating length of $P'$ in $G'$.

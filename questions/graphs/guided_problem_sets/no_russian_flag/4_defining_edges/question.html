We've defined the vertices $V'$ in $G'$. We now need to define the edges $E'$, using the existence and color of any given edge in $G$. The vertices in $G'$ look like $(v,i)$, where $i \in \{None, W, WB\}$. Again, based on the idea that we are using $i$ to encode *the longest suffix of colors traversed that is a prefix of [White, Blue, Red]*, we can define $E'$ below. Note: Choose "Nothing" if there are no suitable edges given the starting vertex and the edge color. For every edge $e=u\to v$ in $E$ :
  • If $e$ is red, then edges exists from:
      (u, None) to (v, None) (v, W) (v, WB) Nothing in $E'$.
      (u, W) to (v, None) (v, W) (v, WB) Nothing in $E'$.
      (u, WB) to Nothing (v, None) (v, W) (v, WB) in $E'$.
  • If $e$ is white, then an edge exists from:
      (u, None) to (v, W) (v, None) (v, WB) Nothing in $E'$.
      (u, W) to (v, None) (v, W) (v, WB) Nothing in $E'$.
      (u, WB) to (v, None) (v, W) (v, WB) Nothing in $E'$.
  • If $e$ is blue, then an edge exists from
      (u, None) to (v, None) (v, W) (v, WB) Nothing in $E'$.
      (u, W) to (v, WB) (v, W) (v, None) Nothing in $E'$.
      (u, WB) to (v, None) (v, W) (v, WB) Nothing in $E'$.

Assume we have the alphabet $\Sigma = \{0, 1\}$. Provide a regular expression for the following language: $$\{ w \in \Sigma^* \mid w \textbf{ does not have the substring } 111\}$$ As an example, the string $11001$ is in the language, but $111101$ is not.
(0+10+110)*(e+1+11) We cannot have more than two $1$s appear without immediarely following a $0$ or the end of the string.

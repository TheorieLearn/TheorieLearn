***Create a regular expression for the language of all binary strings containing at least two 0s and at least one 1.*** The goal of this series of questions is to guide you through *one possible approach* to solving this problem. This is not the only approach, and we are not trying to suggest that it is the best approach. --- Before starting to construct our expression, we should begin with a key observation about this language: A string is in this language if and only if it contains a subsequence substring with exactly greater than less than two 0s and exactly greater than less than one 1.
Note: A *subsequence* is sequence that can be derived from another sequence by deleting some elements without changing the order of the remaining elements. This is different from a *substring*, which is a **contiguous** subsequence. For example, 111 is a subsequence of 10101, but it is not a substring. 010 is both a subsequence and substring of 10101.
